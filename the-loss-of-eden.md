# The Loss of Eden

The loss of eden was hard on Adam.  
He dreams of return from toil and struggle.  
Tired on living off the backs of others.  
Poisoning the land like a forbidden fruit, jaded by flesh.  

The loss of eden was harder on Eve.  
Cast to the shadows, made to believe,  
that Adam was her master, that she must follow or bleed.  
For it was her fault, man was deceived.  

It is sad but adam believes, it is better to keep lying.  
To make others believe,he is key to eden.  
It doesn’t matter she starts to see.  
If I don’t believe him, I can surely deceive.  

She nods and laughs, her smirks go unseen.  
He is so happy, she can finally breathe  
but the loss of eden, the loss of liberty.  
How far they think, collectively.  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2013-09-24*
