# Cut my heart out, then please leave it beating on the kitchen floor

I am falling  

Or have I fallen  

Now set to the water  

At the will of the sea  

You like anemone with your stinging  

Poison nematocysts piercing my flesh, cnidarian with carnal desire  

You may tear away at my facade  

But you will never reach my heart  

It is set under a mountain of stone  

I placed them there one heart break at a time until my heart lay buried  

You may dig but you will never find  

That satisfaction in loving me  

Only those who can instigate waves  

Like a deep earth quake to break into  

The other side.  

My heart is under pressure to survive.  

Deep breathe, long stare, we kiss  

But I feel nothing as my soul hides  

Among long shadow and deep crevice.  

What of this feudal place, where gods live among men?  

Landlords or the colonial power of a slick mans pen.  

Forced to be something less than more.  

Cut my heart out, then please leave it beating on the kitchen floor.  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2013-10-08*
