# Pardon Me My Love

Pardon me my love  

For letting my heart drift so far  

Into a distant land, from this bleeding heart  

I had my heart cut open, expecting cornelian beauty.  

But what I saw, as my eyes were graced.  

Not a heart, or gemstone, no musical beats.  

but interlocking circles, arranged geometrically  

so perfect their ratio, it became clear to me  

This heart is not mine, or for that matter yours,  

alone. Interlocking circles show those who do not know  

we may all be connected, somewhere down the road.  

If it all was easy it would not take heart break or sharp knife  

to open us up to what is sacred  

to pleasantly remind,  

all the breaking heart stones,  

we are more than timelines.  

So pardon me my love  

for letting my heart drift so far  

into a distant land, from this bleeding heart  

I had my heart cut open to see, you are not that far.  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2013-08-12*
