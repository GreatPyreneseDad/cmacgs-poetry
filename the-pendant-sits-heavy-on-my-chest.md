# The Pendant Sits Heavy on My Chest

I wish I had a pendant to wear around my neck  

This pendant would be magical  

connected,  

to my undying soul  

It would light up with my mood  

To give insight on my attitude  

When people ask me what does my pendant do,  

I will respond with only the truth.  

To have no fear of hiding, this gift from beyond  

only a dream rising, or a stone in a pond  

When I am injured let it glow purple,  

Rich lavender hue, I am hurt for the hurtful.  

When I am gentle let it glow green,  

but when I am unsettled let it glow brighter.  

Although in my my past life I have been a fighter  

I know that my love is far and beyond mightier.  

When I am sad let it glow blue,  

but for deeper sadness let it glow through  

Sadness for the world at sight,  

for the ways I fail to do whats right.  

Convicted feelings should glow bright red,  

To keep me from hiding my eyes from what god said.  

When people ask me what does my pendant do,  

I will respond with only the truth.  

My pendant is a gift, a test for me to keep,  

But it does not really exist  

just figurtively.  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2012-01-21*
