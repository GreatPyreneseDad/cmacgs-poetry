# Heart Bleeds Out

When my heartbreaks, it shatters  

No longer whole the pieces melt into a puddle  

When I see your profile picture occupied by another brown head  

My heart evaporates from the floor  

You really had a hold on me  

Planning so many potential ways to find your heart next to mine  

I spend most of my life trying to decide  

If I can really choose to die  

If it would just rain all day  

To wash away my pain  

I would sit cross-legged under the cold crystals  

Maybe my heart will fall back into my chest  

It all started online  

Then La Vie in Rose had our hearts aligned  

Now I am feeling blind but for god’s hands  

Guiding my heart, even through the start  

I never have been stuck like glue  

Forever concerned with what is not my own  

Feelings that is, feelings for you  

I have an overly sensitive empathy bone.  

Now my heartbreaks, it shatters  

No longer hurt the pieces fall numb and lifeless  

When I see them fall and to dry into prunes  

The hole in my chest fills with sharp metal  

Sand pours into my mouth, I am choking on my own emotion  

Life’s motion has put my metal heart in my stomach  

I cannot breath, and I prefer the pain of hunger  

To the pain inside my heartless chest  

But I am meant for this harsh weather  

Without it I would not be better  

It does not keep it from hurting  

But the hurt is meant for me and I will be selfish with it  

I will wear it on my sleeve  

Like a tattoo by a breaking heart  

Illegible and scared, will I ever float again  

I can’t do this anymore.  

If you find me on the floor  

In a puddle trying to drown  

Let me be, I am trying to drink my heart  

Off the floor, splinters in my lips  

Would you kiss this?  

I would turn and run from my reflection  

Headed so far in the opposite direction  

Most have, now it’s up to you.  

The doctors put pins in my chest  

So when I breathe I can feel the prick  

Sharp pain inside my chest  

To remind me I am still alive.”  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2012-02-14*
