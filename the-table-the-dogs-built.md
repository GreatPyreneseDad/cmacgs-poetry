# The table the dogs built

Fresh air is missing from this hotbox  

Each breath drying out our lungs  

That illusion of saliva  

The stark realization that our mouths are so dry that we can barely pull our air holes open.  

What happened to us?  

A people once salivating for freedom  

Now but dogs in the dust and mud  

Fighting over scraps thrown from the table we built.  

A headless behemoth stands where the shining daughter of liberty once had.  

Her eyes gone black on a head that has been struck away.  

But non of the dogs can remember when the process of domestication  

had occurred.  

But they all prefer the warm house to cold porch or being chained to a rock  

So they forgot how to bark.  

They wouldn’t dare bite those at the table, for fear and a legitimate concern for their lives.  

The chairs creek under the growing weight of those at the table.  

The constant sound of jaws chewing and the sounds of fine metals cutting across ivory plates perks the ears of the dogs.  

I am locked in this hotbox  

Wondering when that storm will pull in that one gust of fresh air.  

Waiting to feel the wetness of rain being carried on the wind.  

Knowing I may never live another day as free as men have once been.  

---

*Christopher MacGregor — 2013-07-31*
