# The neutron on the corner

If I were a paranoid schizophrenic…  

He says this with a concerning tone to his crackling voice.  

The anxiety that accompanies his existence drives him to sit in the cold.  

White and grey ash falls on his body.  

It washes him of his guilt.  

The very thing that drives his goal.  

He cannot think in a straight line anymore.  

Fragments of thought explode into smaller fragments.  

As the ash stains his fingertips he thinks about eating, fucking, baseball.  

The story less character passes by him in his eternal withdrawal from reality.  

So many just a footstep away, but in his eyes this distance is irreconcilable.  

He floats in an ocean of charged particles; he sinks and floats as a neutral entity.  

But he is not neutral, only the sum of his actions.

*Christopher MacGregor — 2012-01-20*
